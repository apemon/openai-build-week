# Automatically submit revalidated Decision Batches

Spec Grill V3 treats each Product Manager confirmation as the sole approval for its asynchronous entry, whether a Decision Summary or permitted deferral, then automatically submits one ordered Decision Batch as soon as the preceding validated revision applies and every included entry passes fresh Brain-owned permit revalidation. The Product Manager may undo confirmation only until batch creation begins; batch creation atomically locks its one to three entries. Batch entries remain request-local and do not join the durable confirmed-turn history until a validated complete batch revision applies. A retryable provider/stream failure preserves the exact locked batch for explicit retry; invalid output after repair or explicit abandonment applies none of it, appends no turns, and produces truthful Not Applied outcomes. This avoids a second ambiguous approval click while retaining individual confirmation evidence, exact batch membership, one authoritative request in flight, and complete-snapshot replacement. Contradictory Decision Summaries remain separate confirmed provenance sources, and the Brain must expose and prioritize their unresolved contradiction rather than choose, merge, or downgrade either statement.
