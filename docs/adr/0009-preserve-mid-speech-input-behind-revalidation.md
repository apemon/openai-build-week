# Preserve mid-speech input behind revalidation

When a validated Brain revision arrives after an accepted speech-start event but before finalized transcription, Spec Grill V3 will let transcription finish locally so the Product Manager's speech is not discarded, while disabling the microphone and marking the captured work Revalidation Pending. The captured text cannot produce a confirmable Decision Summary or reach the Brain until the Question Permit is freshly revalidated; valid work resumes against a fresh permit, while invalid work becomes copyable `Not applied` wording and never mutates the Specification. If the revision arrives during prompt playback or listening before speech begins, Spec Grill stops playback and microphone activity immediately and revalidates first; identical freshly approved prompt identity and wording resume listening without replay, while changed permit or wording must be presented and spoken again before capture. If a revision arrives during Decision Summary editing, the application preserves the text, disables confirmation, and revalidates before either rebasing the same editor or moving the wording to Not Applied with `Reuse wording`. Correctness depends on immutable exchange, permit, and cancellation-epoch identifiers plus local rejection of late events, not on best-effort provider cancellation.
