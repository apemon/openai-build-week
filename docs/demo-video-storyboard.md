# Spec Grill — three-minute V3.1 demo storyboard

Target length: 3:00. Capture at 1280×720 or larger in current desktop Chrome using non-confidential content. Keep Live, Prepared, and any local experimental provenance visible and distinct.

## 0:00–0:20 — Product and authority boundary

**Picture:** Start screen and the two mode choices.

**Narration:** “Spec Grill turns vague product intent into a traceable Specification one consequential decision at a time. The Brain alone owns the Specification, roadmap, dependencies, and Interview Window. Prepared Demo is deterministic and never presented as Live AI.”

**Show:** privacy notice, Live configuration state, `Live AI`, and `Prepared demo • no AI call`.

## 0:20–0:42 — Reviewed project context

**Picture:** Prepare bundled `team-billing-project-brief.md`, review statement-level sources, and confirm the digest.

**Narration:** “Only Product Manager-confirmed digest wording becomes Confirmed Input. Original bytes and full extraction stay outside the reload checkpoint.”

**Show:** source references, coverage, warnings, and explicit confirmation.

## 0:42–1:12 — Natural Answer Intake

**Picture:** Show the prepared Brain-authored Answer Aspects, one missing aspect, the prepared clarification, and the editable Answer Summary. Edit the summary before confirming it.

**Narration:** “A transcript is not a decision and does not become a draft immediately. The Communicator assesses only the Brain-authored aspects from temporary Answer Intake, asks one bounded clarification at a time, and proposes an editable summary. Only the exact Product Manager-confirmed summary reaches the Brain.”

**Show:** `Answer Intake · non-authoritative`, Covered/Missing labels, one prepared clarification, `Answer Summary`, and `Send confirmed summary to Brain`. Keep `Prepared demo • no AI call` visible.

## 1:12–1:48 — Bounded Async Interview

**Picture:** Confirm the prepared Answer Summary. Show sticky Prepared Brain Status, then the two-permit window. Confirm the first editable Decision Summary; show the second detailed question only after promotion and confirm it separately.

**Narration:** “The Brain may approve up to three pairwise-independent permits, but the Communicator presents one decision at a time. Confirming the first may promote the next unused permit while the same Brain request continues. Every Decision Summary remains editable and non-authoritative until individually confirmed.”

**Show:** one active prompt, one future topic label, visible clarification exchange, `Confirm decision and continue`, Decision Tray states, and no competing second prompt.

## 1:48–2:12 — Truthful status and revision barrier

**Picture:** Advance the fixture clock to `Taking longer than usual`. Then show the authoritative revision apply before job revalidation.

**Narration:** “Persistent status comes only from content-free lifecycle evidence—never percentages or invented stages. The response barrier sees the currently promoted job, then the complete authoritative revision applies first. One prepared decision remains valid; the other becomes dependency-invalidated Not Applied with reusable wording.”

**Show:** elapsed/last-verified values, `Revision applied`, `Ready to apply`, `Not Applied`, Brain-owned reason, and `Reuse wording`.

## 2:12–2:32 — Atomic batch and reload privacy

**Picture:** Show automatic one-entry prepared batch application. Cut to the mocked reload fixture: no automatic request, then `Revalidate restored decisions`, then the separate `Submit restored decisions` action.

**Narration:** “Individual confirmation authorizes an exact entry only after fresh revalidation. The ordered batch applies atomically. Reload restores at most three confirmed entries—never Answer Intake, drafts, transcripts, assessment output, lifecycle state, or automatic submission.”

**Show:** Applying → Applied, unchanged last valid Specification during work, and both explicit reload gates.

## 2:32–2:47 — Final Review and export

**Picture:** Final Review and downloaded Markdown.

**Narration:** “Decision Tray drafts and Not Applied wording stay out of the authoritative export. Applied work appears only through the validated Specification and provenance.”

**Show:** `ready_with_follow_ups`, Next Actions, Prepared warning, IDs/status/source references, and absence of Decision Tray wording.

## 2:47–3:00 — Verification and honest limits

**Picture:** Passing lint, typecheck, unit/integration, Playwright, and build output; finish on Prepared Final Review.

**Narration:** “Codex implemented V3.1 across frozen contracts, Brain-authored aspects, identity-safe Answer Intake, sequential permit promotion, the accessible Prepared experience, and independent verification. Automated checks use mocks, fake media, and synthetic fixtures. Physical voice timing, advanced media races, deployment streaming duration, and provider retention remain separately verified only when their manual checks actually pass.”
